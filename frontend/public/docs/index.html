SaaS Merchant Guide

Accept USDC subscriptions in minutes

AuthOnce gives your SaaS product a recurring billing layer that requires no smart contract knowledge. Share a pay link, receive USDC. Everything else is automatic.

No code required to get started. Create a product, share your pay link, and receive your first payment — all from the dashboard. Webhooks and the REST API are available when you're ready to go deeper.

Quickstart

From zero to first payment in four steps:

1

Apply as a founding merchant

Go to authonce.io and submit your application. The first 10 approved merchants pay zero protocol fees for 3 months.

2

Connect your wallet

Your merchant wallet is where USDC lands when a payment executes. Connect MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, or any EVM-compatible wallet. This address is public — it's your on-chain identity as a merchant.

3

Create a subscription product

Set a name, price, billing interval (weekly / monthly / yearly), and which payment methods you accept. Takes under 2 minutes.

4

Share your pay link

Every product gets a unique URL: authonce.io/pay/your-name/your-product. Send it by email, embed it in your app, or put it in a button. Subscribers click, connect a wallet or enter a card, and they're subscribed.

The pay page is fully responsive and works on any device. Subscribers choose their payment method on arrival — no friction, no account required.

Payment methods

AuthOnce supports two flows that you control at the product level:

Crypto wallet (USDC, EURC, USDT, DAI)

The subscriber connects a wallet and signs a single EIP-2612 permit. No gas required. From that point, AuthOnce's keeper bot pulls the subscription amount on each billing date directly to your wallet. The subscriber never needs to approve again.

Zero chargebacks. On-chain payments are cryptographically final. There is no chargeback mechanism — once the payment executes, it's settled.

Card (fiat)

The subscriber pays with a credit or debit card through your connected payment processor account. You receive the payment in your bank account in the currency you configured. AuthOnce charges its 0.5% fee as an application fee on top of the processor's standard fee.

Card payments require setup. Connect your payment processor account from Dashboard → Settings → Payments before enabling card as a payment method.

Grace period & dunning

When a payment fails — either because the subscriber's wallet has insufficient balance or their approval expired — AuthOnce does not immediately cancel the subscription. Instead, it enters a grace period.

During the grace period:

  • The keeper retries the payment once every 24 hours
  • The subscriber receives an email notification immediately, and a reminder every 48 hours
  • You receive a notification that the payment failed and the grace period end date
  • The subscription status shows as Grace in your dashboard

If the subscriber tops up their wallet (or re-approves), the next daily retry will succeed and billing continues normally. If the grace period ends with no successful payment, the subscription expires.

Grace period1 – 30 days7 days
Retry frequencyEvery 24 hoursFixed

Set grace period per product, not per subscriber. Configure it when creating or editing the product. All subscribers on that product share the same grace period setting.

Webhooks

Webhooks let your backend react in real time to subscription events. AuthOnce sends a signed HTTPS POST to your endpoint within seconds of each event.

Configure your webhook endpoint

From Dashboard → Settings → Webhooks, enter your endpoint URL. AuthOnce will sign every request with HMAC-SHA256 using your webhook secret.

Every webhook payload follows the same structure:

{
  "event":     "payment.success",
  "timestamp": 1750000000,
  "data": {
    "subscription_id": "42",
    "merchant_address": "0xbb6d...",
    "vault_address":    "0xd889...",
    "amount_usdc":      "10.00",
    "merchant_received_usdc": "9.95",
    "tx_hash":        "0xecb0...",
    "status":         "active"
  }
}

Webhook events

subscription.created

A new subscriber authorised a recurring payment. Billing will begin on the next billing date.

payment.success

A payment was pulled successfully. USDC is now in your wallet. Includes transaction hash.

payment.failed

Payment failed — insufficient balance or expired approval. Grace period has started. Includes grace period end date.

payment.upcoming

A subscriber has a payment due in 3 days. Fired once per billing cycle as an advance notice.

subscription.cancelled

A subscriber cancelled. No further payments will be collected. Merchants cannot trigger this event.

subscription.expired

Grace period ended without a successful payment. Subscription is permanently closed.

subscription.resumed

A subscription that was in grace period recovered — subscriber topped up and the retry succeeded.

subscription.expiring

You set a product expiry date. This fires when the 30-day notice was sent to the subscriber.

Signature verification

Every webhook includes an X-AuthOnce-Signature header. Always verify it before processing the event — it protects you from spoofed requests.

// Node.js example
const crypto = require("crypto");

function verifyWebhook(rawBody, signature, secret) {
  const expected = "sha256=" + crypto
    .createHmac("sha256", secret)
    .update(rawBody)
    .digest("hex");
  return crypto.timingSafeEqual(
    Buffer.from(expected),
    Buffer.from(signature)
  );
}

// In your Express route:
app.post("/webhook/authonce", (req, res) => {
  const sig = req.headers["x-authonce-signature"];
  if (!verifyWebhook(req.rawBody, sig, process.env.AUTHONCE_WEBHOOK_SECRET)) {
    return res.status(401).send("Unauthorized");
  }
  // Safe to process
  const { event, data } = req.body;
  // ...
  res.status(200).send("OK");
});

Cancellations

Merchants cannot cancel subscriptions. Only the subscriber or their designated guardian can cancel. This is a deliberate protocol design — it guarantees subscribers that merchants cannot cut off their access or manipulate billing.

If you need to stop billing a subscriber, contact them directly and ask them to cancel from their subscriber portal at authonce.io/my-subscriptions. Alternatively, you can pause your product from the dashboard — no new payments will be attempted while paused.

Tax exports

From Dashboard → Payments → Export, you can download:

  • CSV — all payments with date, subscriber, amount in USDC and EUR equivalent, transaction hash
  • XLSX — same data as a spreadsheet, with a separate guide tab explaining the columns for your accountant
